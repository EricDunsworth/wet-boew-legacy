Global Accessibility

In response to the growing number of individuals who are blind or visually impaired around the world, governments have begun to institute accessibility legislation and standards to ensure the right to access and inclusion for their citizens. This leadership by governments both domestically and internationally is designed to remove barriers and ensure equal opportunities for people with disabilities.

Accessibil-IT understands there are unique differences between domestic and the multitude of international global accessibility legislation. Our knowledge is critical for providing fully accessible and compliant communications to your target audience at home or abroad.

Missing the Accessibility Mark

Many organizations and governments around the world don't yet realize their responsibilities for ensuring their products, services, built environment and information is accessible for people with disabilities.

Organizations that ignore their responsibilities to ensure easy access to their offerings, have faced repercussions ranging from negative press, to multi-million dollar lawsuits.

Accessibility includes more than ramps and elevators. It is critical that your organization, from executives to front line staff, recognizes that a holistic approach is required for accessibility to be culturally entrenched.

When an organization ignores the repeated requests from staff or clients for accommodations to improve accessibility, and ease of access to their products or services, these individuals are often left with no other option but to pursue legal avenues to remove said barriers.

Legal Precedents

The following list provides summaries of legal proceedings against organizations who have faced ramifications for ignoring requests for accessibility.

  • Donna Jodhan v. Attorney General of Canada (Statistics Canada)
  • Stark & Stark v. Attorney General of Canada (Royal Bank of Canada)
  • NFB v. Target Corporation
  • State of New York v. HSBC
  • California Council of the Blind v. Bank of America
  • NFB & ACB v. Arizona State University
  • NFB v. United Airlines
